About Firass

After graduating from high school in Abbotsford, Firass attended Saint Mary’s University in Halifax on a football scholarship.

Upon completion of his Bachelor of Commerce Degree, Firass moved back to his home town in Abbotsford. He worked in a corporate setting for 5 years. However, his passion for fitness was too strong and he decided to pursue a career in that field.

Firass completed his OPEX CCP and has been a Fitness Coach for almost a decade now. In that time he’s helped many people achieve their health and wellness goals through a holistic approach.

When he’s not coaching, Firass enjoys working out (of course!), and spending time with his two young children and wife.

Dr. Yafa Elseri

Owner / Chiropractor

Book AbbotsfordBook Chilliwack

About Yafa

Dr. Elseri commenced her path to becoming a chiropractor by graduating from the University of Alberta in 2006 with a Bachelors of Science degree.  Following graduation she moved to San Jose, California where she obtained her Doctor of Chiropractic degree (Cum Laude) from Palmer College of Chiropractic West in 2009. She is a member of the Phi Delta Chiropractic Honor Society for outstanding academic achievement.
‍
Dr. Elseri has practiced in Alberta for 5 years and has been practicing in British Columbia for the past 3 years.  She is keenly focused on each individual patient and strives to provide personalized care based off each patient's needs and goals.  She is certified in Active Release Techniques and is a Graston Technique provider.  In addition to traditional chiropractic adjustments, Dr. Elseri treats soft tissue as an integral part of proper spinal biomechanics. She provides safe, gentle and effective care for a wide range of patients from the small child with postural concerns, to the elite athlete, to her oldest patient who is 91 years old. She recognizes that we are all unique and what works for one person may not work for the next.
‍
Outside of practicing Dr. Elseri has always had a passion for athletics.  She played competitive ringette for 14 years and was selected to play for the US National Ringette Team at the World Championships.  Currently Dr. Elseri still makes fitness a priority while enjoying motherhood with her daughter and son.

Logan McDonald

About Logan

Logan McDonald was born and raised in Abbotsford, BC, and is proud to serve the local community he calls home. After beginning his studies in Kinesiology at the University of the Fraser Valley, Logan went on to complete his Bachelor of Science and Doctor of Chiropractic degrees at the University of Western States, along with a Master’s degree in Sports Medicine.

Emma Swanson

Kinesiolgist

About Emma

Emma graduated from the University of the Fraser Valley in December 2023 with a Bachelor of Kinesiology, specialising in Exercise Science.
‍
Her journey into kinesiology began through her own experience as a competitive dancer. After sustaining multiple injuries and working through rehabilitation to return to dance, Emma discovered her passion for exercise rehabilitation and the power of movement.
‍

Today, Emma helps clients achieve their goals by building sustainable, healthy exercise habits and developing self-efficacy. She works extensively with ICBC clients, supporting their return to work, school, and daily activities. In2025, she also became a certified Pre- and Post-Natal Exercise Specialist, inspired by her growing passion for helping expecting and new mothers feel strong, confident, and supported.
‍
Emma believes that movement is medicine. Her approach is taking things one step at a time and cultivating lasting habits to enhance health, recovery, and overall well-being.

About Gagandeep

Gagandeep Gill was born and raised in Abbotsford, British Columbia and has chosen to remain in Abbotsford to practice as a Doctor of Chiropractic. His interest in this career began after a car accident during his high school years where chiropractic care helped in his recovery. Gagandeep has always had a passion for the healthcare field and choosing this route allowed him to contribute both his knowledge and passion to help others in the community.

Gagandeep completed his Bachelor of Science in Biology with a Cellular, Molecular and Genetics concentration in 2018. In his last years of high school, Gagandeep was involved in a serious car accident that left him with a moderate amount of low back pain. He was then referred to a chiropractor to help alleviate the back pain as well as learning how to manage the back pain by himself. This incident sparked Gagandeep’s interest into making chiropractic care his profession.

Gagandeep completed his Doctor of Chiropractic degree at the University of Western States with the graduating class of 2022 (Summa Cum Laude). He also completed a Master of Science in Sports Medicine as well as a Bachelor of Science in Human Biology. Gagandeep has worked with and treated many patients including athletes on high school sports teams. He has worked with patients of all ages and is skilled in personalizing treatment based on differences among every patient.

Outside of practising, Gagandeep enjoys incorporating exercise such as weightlifting and playing basketball. Gagandeep has a huge passion for cars. In his spare time, Gagandeep enjoys building, repairing and customizing cars and trucks.

Jessica Bugra

Acupuncturist

Book AbbotsfordBook Chilliwack

About Jessica

Jessica Bugra is a licensed acupuncturist from the Fraser Valley. She is passionate about raising awareness of advancing, well-researched and tested alternative therapies. Her goal is to help enhance your well-being and inspire you to live a more mindful life. Jessica pursued Traditional Chinese Medicine at the PCU College of Holistic Medicine. She uses the foundations of Traditional Chinese Medicine to safely and effectively support healing.

TCM is a system of healing that is over 2000 years old. It uses a sophisticated method of diagnosis that factors in age, sex, genetics, diet, lifestyle, emotions environment, and even the current season to determine the cause of symptoms. We observe the body in a dynamic state, including observations of pulse, tongue, the physical body (skin, hair, nails, strength of limbs, body’s alignment etc.) and vitality. The diagnosis and treatment is unique to the individual. The purpose of TCM is to treat not only the symptom but the root of the disease, reinforce the correct energy flow or qi, eliminate anything in the body that does not serve it, and regulate homeostasis.
